Uzbek Samsas

Description

Samsas are traditional Central Asian pastries, typically baked in a tandoor or oven, with a flaky crust and savory fillings like meat, onion, or spiced potato. Popular in Uzbek cuisine, they are often triangular and served hot as a street food or snack alongside tea.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or melted butter
  • Extra flour for dusting
  • Optional 3 tbsp melted butter or lamb fat (for brushing rolled dough layers)

For the filling:

  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced very small (5mm cubes)
  • 1 large onion
  • 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds or ground cumin
  • 0.5 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional chopped fresh coriander

For finishing:

  • 1 egg yolk + 1 tsp milk (for egg wash)
  • Optional sesame seeds

Steps

Make the dough:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add warm water and oil. Mix until a soft dough forms.
  2. Knead on a floured surface for 10 minutes until smooth.
  3. Cover and let rest for 30-60 minutes at room temperature.

Make the filling:

  1. In a bowl, combine diced potatoes, onion, oil, cumin, black pepper, and salt.
  2. Mix well and let sit for at least 20 minutes so the flavors meld. (Optional: add herbs at this stage.)

Roll and shape:

  1. Divide dough into 2 balls. Roll out the first ball into a very thin rectangle (as thin as possible).
  2. Brush with melted butter or oil. Roll into a tight log (like a jelly roll). Slice into 6 equal pieces.
  3. Take each slice and roll flat into a circle (~12-15 cm).
  4. Place 2-3 tbsp of filling in the center. Fold into a triangle or round shape and seal the edges well.

Bake:

  1. Place samsas on a baking tray lined with parchment.
  2. Brush tops with egg wash. Sprinkle with sesame or nigella seeds if using.
  3. Bake at 200°C (390°F) for 30-40 minutes until golden brown.

Serve:

Let rest for a few minutes out of the oven. Serve warm with tea, yogurt, or a light salad.
