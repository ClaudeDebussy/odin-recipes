Uzbek Samsas

Description

Samsas are traditional Central Asian pastries, typically baked in a tandoor or oven, with a flaky crust and savory fillings like meat, onion, or spiced potato. Popular in Uzbek cuisine, they are often triangular and served hot as a street food or snack alongside tea.

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil or melted butter
  • Extra flour for dusting
  • Optional 3 tbsp melted butter or lamb fat (for brushing rolled dough layers)

For the filling:

  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced very small (5mm cubes)
  • 1 large onion
  • 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds or ground cumin
  • 0.5 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional chopped fresh coriander

For finishing:

  • 1 egg yolk + 1 tsp milk (for egg wash)
  • Optional sesame seeds
