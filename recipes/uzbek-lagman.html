Uzbek Lagman

Description

A beloved Central Asian noodle soup or stir-fry that blends hand-pulled noodles with a rich, spiced beef and vegetable sauce. There are many regional variations (soupy or dry), but this version is a classic semi-brothy style often served in Uzbekistan.

Ingredients

For the noodles:

  • 2.5 cups (300g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 egg (optional, for richer dough)
  • ~.5 cup (120ml) warm water
  • 1-2 tbsp vegetable oil (for resting noodles)

For the sauch (lagman base)

  • 300-400g (10-14 oz) beef or lamb, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large carrot, julienned or diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, peeled and chopped (or 2 tbsp tomato paste)
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and cubed
  • 0.5 daikon or white radish (optional), julienned
  • 1 chili pepper (optional), finely chopped
  • 1-1.5 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups (1 liter) water or beef broth
  • Soy sauce or vinegar to taste (optional, for final adjustment)
  • Fresh cilantro or scallions (for garnish)

Steps

Make the noodles:

  1. Combine flour, salt, egg (if using), and water to form a stiff but pliable dough.
  2. Knead for 10 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rest for 30-60 minutes.
  3. Roll into long ropes and coat with oil. Let rest again (30 min).
  4. Stretch into thin noodles (or roll and cut like fettuccine). Boil for 2-3 minutes in salted water. Rinse and toss with oil.

Make the sauce:

  1. Heat oil in a pot. Sear the beef until browned.
  2. Add onions and garlic, cook until soft.
  3. Stir in carrot, bell pepper, and chili. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add tomatoes (or paste), cook until soft.
  5. Add potato and daikon. Pour in water or broth. Season with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  6. Simmer for 30-45 minutes until meat and veggies are tender and the sauce thickens slightly.

Serve:

  1. Place a handful of noodles into a bowl.
  2. Ladle sauce and broth over the top.
  3. Garnish with fresh herbs or a dash of soy sauce or vinegar if desired.
