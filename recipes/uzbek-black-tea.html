Uzbek Black Tea (Chorny Chai)

Description

A simple, traditional recipe for Uzbek black tea (chorny chai)—a central part of Uzbek hospitality, typically served strong, hot, and without milk, often in a small bowl called a piyola.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon loose-leaf black tea (e.g. Ceylon or Chinese black tea)
  • 3 cups (700 ml) water
  • Opetional: lemon slices, sugar, or dried apricots for serving

Steps

  1. Boil the water in a kettle or saucepan.
  2. Warm the teapot by rinsing it with a small amount of hot water and discarding it.
  3. Add loose tea leaves to the warm teapot (about 1 tsp per person).
  4. Pour boiling water over the tea leaves. Cover and steep for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Traditional Uzbek method: Pour tea into a piyola (tea bowl), then back into the teapot—repeat 2-3 times to mix and aerate.
  6. Serve immediately in small bowls or cups, optionally with sugar, lemon slices, or sweets on the side.
